## Carbon Credit & Indian Carbon Market (ICM) — Straightforward Summary

This is a **condensed overview of everything discussed so far** about carbon credits, agroforestry opportunities, and carbon business models in India.

---

# 1. What a Carbon Credit Is

- **1 carbon credit = 1 metric ton of CO₂ reduced or removed from the atmosphere.**
    
- Projects that reduce emissions or capture carbon generate these credits.
    
- Companies buy credits to **offset their emissions and meet climate targets**.
    

Typical project types:

- Tree planting / forestry
    
- Agroforestry
    
- Soil carbon farming
    
- Renewable energy
    
- Methane capture
    
- Biochar
    

---

# 2. Carbon Markets

There are two main types.

### Compliance Market

- Government regulated.
    
- Companies must meet emission limits.
    
- Example: **Indian Carbon Market (ICM).**
    

### Voluntary Carbon Market

- Companies buy credits voluntarily for ESG and net-zero commitments.

# 4. Indian Carbon Market (ICM)

India launched **Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS) 2023**.

Key institutions:

- Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE)
    
- Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (CERC)
    
- Ministry of Power
    

Carbon credits are expected to trade through:

- Indian Energy Exchange (IEX)
    
- Power Exchange India
    

Goal:

- Reduce emissions intensity **45% by 2030**
    
- Reach **Net Zero by 2070**

# 5. Agroforestry – Biggest Opportunity

Agroforestry = **Trees + Crops on the same land**

Benefits:

- High carbon capture
    
- Improved soil health
    
- Additional farm income
    

Typical carbon capture:

```
1 hectare agroforestry
≈ 5–15 carbon credits per year
```

Example:

```
10 hectares land
≈ 80 credits/year
$25 per credit
≈ $2000/year (~₹1.6 lakh)
```

Common tree species:

- Bamboo
    
- Teak
    
- Neem
    
- Mango
    
- Eucalyptus
    
- Sandalwood
    

---

# 6. Carbon Credit Calculation Example

Average tree carbon capture:

|Tree|CO₂ per year|
|---|---|
|Bamboo|~35 kg|
|Teak|~25 kg|
|Neem|~20 kg|

Example:

```
200 trees/hectare
× 25 kg CO₂
= 5000 kg CO₂
= ~5 carbon credits/year
```

# 7. Carbon Project Lifecycle

Typical steps:

```
1. Project identification
2. Baseline carbon measurement
3. Project Design Document (PDD)
4. Monitoring
5. Third-party verification
6. Carbon credit issuance
7. Carbon credit sale
```

---

# 8. MRV System

MRV = **Monitoring, Reporting, Verification**

Technologies used:

- Satellite monitoring
    
- Remote sensing
    
- Soil carbon testing
    
- AI carbon modeling
    
- GPS mapping
    

MRV ensures the project actually reduces carbon.
